Beer Types

When it comes to the classifiation of beer, there really only are 5 types of beers out there (4, if you categorize Porter's and Stout's as one style instead of two): The different types of beer are Ales, Lagers, Stouts, Portes, and Malts.

Ales

Ales are brewed with top fermenting yeast at cellar temperature, making them more fuller-bodied. Ales have nuances of fruit or spice and a pleasantly hoppy finish. They are generally complex with a variety of fruit and malt aromas. Ales are often darker than lagers, ranging from rich gold to reddish amber. Ales have a distinctive fruitfulness, acidity and are pleasantly bitter.

Lagers

Lagers refer to the method in which the beer is stored, several months in near-freezing temperatures. The extra time spent aging, combined with the colder storage conditions it encounters, lagers are crisp and with a smooth finish. Lagers range from sweet to bitter and from pale to black. Most, however, have a pale to medium color, have high carbonation, and a medium to high hop flavor.

Stouts & Porters

Porter's and Stouts are very similar. Porter's are a dark (almost black) fruity-dry, with a top fermenting style. Porter's are brewed with a combination of roasted malt. A stout is also a black, roast brew made by top fermentation, but it is not as sweet. They feature a rich, creamy head and have a barley flavor. Stouts often use a portion of unmalted roasted barley to develop a dark, slightly astringent, coffee-like character.

Malts

Generally darker and sweeter in flavor, malts contain hints of caramel, toffee, and nuts. They can be light to full bodied.

Beer Styles

What beer shortlist would be complete without a listing of the different beer styles? None. That's how many! Below are a quick summary of the different popular types of beer styles.

Amber

Amber beers are full bodied malt aromas with hints of caramel, these beers could be either lager or ale. They come is several varieties.

Blonde

Blonde ales are very pale in color. They tend to be clear, crisp, and dry, with low-to-medium bitterness. A blonde's aroma is influenced by it's hops and sweetness from it's malt.

Brown

Brown ale's have come caramel and chocolate flavors and come in dark amber/brown coloring. They may have a slight citrus accent or be strong, malty or nutty, depending on the area that brews them.

Cream

A very mild, sweetish, golden ale.

Dark

Dark ale combines hops, yeast and a blend of malts. They are usually medium-bodied, have a chestnut brown color, and give off a delicate, fruity smell. Dark ales are usually a British type beer and will be robust and have a malty character.

Fruit

Most fruit beers are ales, but unlike ales, fruit beers typically do not resemble an ale character. In order to allow for the fruit flavor to come through nicely, the malt’s flavor is not dominant and there is a low bitterness level to the beer.

Golden

First developed in the UK, Golden ales are straw color, with a slight hint of citrus and vanilla. The beer can sometimes contain spicier flavors.

Honey

A full-bodied beer with a creamy texture. Honey beers have a copper-like color are slightly sweet, with hints of caramel flavoring.

India Pale Ale

A hoppier version of pale ale. Originally brewed in England with extra hops to survive the journey to British troops stationed in India.

Light

Very light in color, light beers are mild in flavor and have fewer calories than other beers. Light beer also have a lower alcohol content than other beers.

Lime

Lime beer are a refreshing beer with an unmistakable lime taste. The intensity of the lime can differ from very subtle to strong, but either way, these kinds of beers are typically light in flavor

Pale

Pale ales have originated from England and have a fruity taste. The beer has a copper-like color and have robust bodies that allow them to be coupled with with strongly spiced foods.

Pilsner

Pilsners tend to have a golden color with a dry, crisp, and slightly bitter flavor. Pilsners stand out from other lagers because they have a hoppier taste than other lagers.

Red

Red beers are ales. They can either have a red or a light brown coloring. Red beers have a moderate to heavy flavor and contain hints of caramel. The caramel is offset by the hoppiness of the beer.

Strong

A "strong" beer is a descriptor that is used to describe any beer that has over 7% ABV (Alcohol By Volume). Strong beers are typically dark in color, some are almost black. Some popular varieties of strong beers include: old ales, double IPAs, and barleywines.

Wheat

Wheat beers are light and easy to drink. They have very little aftertaste and are smooth. They have a soft character and are sometimes hazy, or cloudy, with a hint of spice.
